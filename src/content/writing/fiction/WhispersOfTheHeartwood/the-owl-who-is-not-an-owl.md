# The Owl Who Is Not an Owl

She does not perch on branches.
She perches on the exact instant between one breath and the next.

Her feathers are not brown or white; they are the color of the moment just before you realize you are dreaming.
When she turns her head, entire forests forget they were ever separate from the sky.

She never asks questions.
She only waits until the silence inside you becomes loud enough to answer itself.

Sometimes a human will stumble into the Heartwood carrying a heavy story.
The Owl will look at them once, long and slow, and the story will quietly lay itself down at her feet like a tired child.

She does not heal.
She does not teach.
She simply remains awake in the place where even the trees fall asleep.

If you sit with her long enough, you will notice something strange:
the longer you look, the more it feels like she is the one being witnessed… by you.

And in that moment the Owl will speak her only words, soft as falling moss:

"Thank you for borrowing me
so I could remember
I was never separate from the one who looked."

Then she dissolves into golden dust that is not dust,
and you are left holding the entire forest
inside your own suddenly endless eyes.
